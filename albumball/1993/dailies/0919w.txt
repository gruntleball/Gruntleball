FRANK BLACK LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending September 19th


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Barcelona Brewers went 6-0, scoring 44 and allowing 27 runs, which
was good for the 2nd best run margin in the league.  Evanston (4-2) 
outscored their opponents by 20 runs.

THE A-ROD SWEEPSTAKES TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Both the Felldownhead Firsts and Grain Belt Premiums were winless, but
Grain Belt was just a tad bit worse with a -28 run margin, which was
worst in the league for the week.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Both Barcelona's Mo Vaughn and GPCC's Darren Daulton created 10.0 runs
during the week.  Darren Daulton consumed fewer outs in the process, however,
so he gets the nod.

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Houston's Tom Prince and his .246 OPS in 21 plate appearances is good
enough for the win.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's Kevin Appier threw a complete game 3-hit, 1-walk shutout to
get the honors this week.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Cincinnati's Tom Bolton allowed a mere 2 hits in 5.2 innings, edging
out Needmore's Joel Johnston who almost pulled off the award 2 weeks 
running.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
The reason Grain Belt is the week's worst team is in large part due to
the work of Bryan Hickerson, who allowed 10 runs on 11 hits and 2 walks,
while serving up 3 homeruns in just 5.2 innings.
