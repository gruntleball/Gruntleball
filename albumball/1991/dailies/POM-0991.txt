Good Team of the Month:
During September, the Butt Mountain Beavis were the hottest team in
the league, with a record of 21-6.  They were also the most dominant,
outscoring the opposition 165-80.

Bad Team of the Month:
The Grain Belt Premiums went 3-24 during September, scoring 92 runs 
but allowing 179, a margin of -87, worst in the league.

Hitter of the Month:
Butt Mountain's Ryne Sandberg was a big reason for their huge September.
Ryne made 26 starts and put up a line of 379/438/747 and created 32.7
runs.  Grain Belt's Bobby Bonilla was runner up with 31.4 runs created.

Bad Hitter of the Month:
Ferdinand's Tim Naehring made 15 starts and had 49 plate appearances.
His OPS was a meager .276 and he created just 0.9 runs for the month.

Starter of the Month:
The other reason for Butt Mountain's huge month were Roger Clemens 
and John Smoltz, both of whom pitched outstanding.  Clemens was the
more dominant of the two, but you could make an argument for either.
Clemens: 51.1 26 11 10 7 43 1.75, Smoltz: 42 30 8 7 16 37 1.50

Reliever of the Month:
Butt Mountain's Mike Schooler appeared in 10 games, threw 17 innings,
and allowed nine hits, two walks and struck out 19.

Bad Pitcher of the Month:
Reykjavik's Bobby Witt made six starts and lasted just 17.2 innings,
allwoing 24 runs.
