TLM: Weekly Awards for the Week of September 16th

Good Team of the Week
The Butt Mountain Beavis went 7-0 and outscored, well, pummelled their
opponents 57-12.  

Bad Team of the Week
The Baraboo Goitermen went 0-6 and were outscored by 28 runs.  The Grain
Belt Premiums went 1-6 and were outscored by 31 runs.  Nod to Baraboo.

Hitter of the Week
Butt Mountain's Brian Downing created 18.1 runs, this season's record, 
and maybe the most RC in a week in league history.  In 35 plate appearances
he batted 500/571/1067 with 4 homeruns, 13 runs scored and 10 RBIs.  
Runners up were Frank Thomas with 14.3 RC and Ken Caminiti with 11.6.

Bad Hitter of the Week
Reykjavik's Ozzie Guillen put up a .174 OPS in 23 plate appearances.

Starter of the Week
Butt Mountain's Roger Clemens pitched two complete games, and though he
allowed two runs in one game, he pitched a shutout in the other, and 
cumulatively, he allowed just 10 hits and three walks, while striking out 15.

Reliever of the Week
Ferdinand's Tom Gordon appeared in three games.  He tossed six innings of
scoreless relief, allowing just two hits and no walks, and struck out five.

Bad pitcher of the Week 
Reykjaviks's Bobby Witt made two starts and lasted a total of 2.1 innings 
during which he allowed 10 runs on 6 hits and nine walks.  Remarkably, no
home runs were issued.
