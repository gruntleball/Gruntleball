Hitters of the Month

Newport's Barry Bonds created 30.6 (!) runs above average with a .568 wOBA and 14 homeruns. He had 138 plate appearances with a batting line of 418/515/918. Waayy back, the runners up were Newport's Richie Sexson with 22.6 wRAA and a wOBA of .524 and Tewksbury's Carlos Delgado (again) with 21.0 wRAA and a .500 wOBA. Sammy Sosa and Nomar Garciaparra rounded out the top five.

Bad hitter of the month was Fallkirk's Adam Kennedy who cost his team 19.3 runs with a .169 woBA. Raul Casanova was bad runner up after "winning" bad hitter for July.

Pitchers of the Month

Barcelona's Pedro Martinez saved his team 26.2 (holy shit!) runs compared to the average pitcher. He pitched 49.0 innings with a wOBA against of only .182 and 64 strikeouts. A fair distance back was McCullers's Tom Glavine who saved 18.5 runs in 47.2 innings with a .231 wOBA against. Another fair distance back was Baraboo's Tim Hudson saved 12.4 runs with a .237 wOBA against in 33.0 innings.

Reliever of the month goes to Brookfield's Danny Graves who saved 10.4 runs above average over 19.0 innings with a .192 wOBA. Next best reliever was Baraboo's Joe Slusarski who saved 10.4 runs, also in 19.0 innings with a .181 wOBA.

The worst pitcher of the month was Century's Chris Carpenter who cost his team 17.6 runs more than an average pitcher, tossing 26.0 innings and coughing up 10 homeruns and with a .476 wOBA. Dinosaur's Steve Trachsel served up 11 homeruns in August, and his pitching cost the team 16.0 runs compared to an average pitcher.
