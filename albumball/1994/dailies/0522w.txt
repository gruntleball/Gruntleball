CROOKED RAIN, CROOKED RAIN LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending May 22nd.


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Tucker Tools Police went 6-1, and had the third best run margin, 
scoring 40 runs and allowing 19. The best run margin belonged to Butt 
Mountain at +26 runs.  Baraboo, by virtue of their potent offense, had
a run margin of +24.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The San Carlos Quentins were 1-6 during the week, scoring 31 runs while
allowing 57.  The Hong Kong Cavaliers (3-4) had the poorest run margin
of -29 runs, as they allowed an altitude-inflated 64 runs during the week.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Baraboo's Kevin Mitchell created 14.1 runs in 29 plate appearances with a
1.557 OPS.  Runners up were Deion Sanders (12.3 RC in only 18 PA), Tony
Gwynn (11.9), Dave Justice (11.1), Brett Butler (10.8) and Kenny Lofton
(10.5)

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Chinatrust's Tim Raines and his 0.098 OPS in 21 plate appearances takes 
the honors.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Garmisch-Partenkirchen's Armando Reynoso allowed only 8 hits and 5 walks
in 17.0 innings.  Yes, he allowed 5 runs, but he was unlucky.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Evanston's Roberto Hernandez allowed no runs on one hit and one walk in
5.2 innings, while striking out four.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Garmisch-Partenkirchen's Mark Langston allowed 15 runs on 18 hits (of 
which five were homeruns) plus five walks in 9.0 innings.
