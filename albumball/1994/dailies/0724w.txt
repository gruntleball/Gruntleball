CROOKED RAIN, CROOKED RAIN LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending July 24th

GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The BUTT MOUNTAIN BEAVIS went 7-0, scoring an average of 4.0 runs per
game and allowing a mere 2.0 runs per game.  The largest run margin 
belonged to Baraboo (63-40 = 23 runs) who also and not surprisingly
led the league in offense.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Both the SAN CARLOS QUENTINS and Garmisch-Partenkirchen Chim-Chim
Shebazzle! went 0-7.  San Carlos allowed 54 runs and GPCC 55 runs, 
but San Carlos scored 28 runs to 31 for GPCC.  The Cincinnati Reds
scored only 24 runs during the week.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Baraboo's JEFF BAGWELL and Ferdinand's BARRY BONDS each created 13.9
runs.  Runners up were Ferdinand's Craig Biggio and Reykjavik's Mike
Piazza with 13.0 RC.  Tewksbury's Chuck Knoblauch created 11.8 runs
during the week.

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
GPCC's HOWARD JOHNSON put up a .176 OPS in 29 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's KEVIN APPIER threw 16 innings and one complete game shutout.
He allowed 7 hits and 2 walks while striking out 18.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Baraboo's MIKE JACKSON threw 4.1 scoresless innings, allowing but one
hit and striking out EIGHT!

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Chinatrust's JERRY DIPOTO made three appearances totaling 3.0 innings.
He surrendered 11 runs on 15 hits plus 4 walks, and surprisingly, NO
homeruns.
