Good Team of the Month:
The Butt Mountain Beavis, despite significant injury issues, led the
league with a 21-5 record during July.  They allowed only 72 runs for
the month, easily leading the league.  Just behind them were the 
Reykjavik 101ers (21-6) and Baraboo Goitermen (20-6).  Baraboo had
both the most potent offense (again...) at 211 runs scored and the 
greatest run margin (again) at +85 runs.

Bad Team of the Month:
The San Carlos Quentins were 6-21 to trail everybody and were helped by
the most runs allowed with 201 and a -68 run margin--all league worsts. 
Felldownhead only scored 80 runs to bring up the rear in offense.   

Hitter of the Month:
Needmore's Ken Griffey, with 40.5 runs created eeked out the award over
Ferdinand's Barry Bonds (40.3 RC).  Rounding out the 30 RC club were 
Baraboo's Jeff Bagwell (39.7) and Kenny Lofton (34.8) and Reykjavik's 
Moises Alou (33.8 RC) and Albert Belle (32.4) and Chinatrust's Paul
O'Neill (31.4).

Bad Hitter of the Month:
Felldownhead's Kein Young made 26 starts and had 99 plate appearances 
and managed a .448 OPS.

Starter of the Month:
Tucker's David Cone made five starts, won four of them and threw 40.2
innings in which he allowed 10 runs on 23 hits plus 13 walks while 
striking out 32.

Reliever of the Month:
Baraboo's Mike Jackson threw 17.0 innings in 15 games.  He allowed 2 runs
on six hits and 3 walks while striking out 26.

Bad Pitcher of the Month:
Evanston's Steve Cooke allowed 26 runs on 42 hits and 3 walks in 20.0 innings
He also served up six homeruns.
