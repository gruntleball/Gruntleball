CROOKED RAIN, CROOKED RAIN LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending May 1st.


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Cincinnati Reds went 7-0 during the week and scored 22 runs
while allowing only 10 (fewest in the league).  The leagues top 
offense was Hong Kong with 56 runs scored (they also tied for the 
most runs allowed with 56 as well).  The league's best run margin 
belonged to Barcelona, who scored 35 and allowed 16 runs.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
It was a bad week for the Gold Soundz division where three teams
went 1-6.  Of those, the Evanston Endorphins had the worst run
differential at -12 runs.  The league-worst run differential 
belonged to the GPCC Shebazzle at -18 runs.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Hong Kong's Frank Thomas created 17.6 runs in 31 plate appearances,
compiling an OPS of 1.748 and a 33.9 RC27 rate.  Six other players
achieved double-digit RC during the week:  Ellis Burks, Wil Cordero,
Greg Vaughn, Chili Davis, Fred McGriff and Tony Fernandez.

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Evanston's Ozzie Guillen put up a big nothing in 17 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's Pedro Martinez pitched 13.0 innings and allowed 2 runs on 
only 4 hits and four walks while striking out 15.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Grain Belt's Armando Benitez pitched 5.0 innings, allowing only one hit
and one walk and struck out six.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Hong Kong's Pete Schourek made two starts and lasted all of 3.1 innings,
in which he allowed 9 runs on 11 hits plus 7 walks.
