WOWEE ZOWEE! LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending June 11th


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FERDINAND MAGELLANS went 6-0, as did the San Carlos Quentins.  
Ferdinand led the league in scoring with 61 runs and San Carlos
led the league in pitching, allowing only 21 runs.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Four teams went 1-5 during the week.  The TUCKER TOOLS POLICE only 
scored 17 runs, to trail the league and allowed 45 runs, which was
also tied for the worst with the Tewksbury Bobs, who were also 1-5.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's BARRY BONDS wins for a second consecutive week, creaging 13.1
runs in 31 plate appearances with a batting line of 400/516/960.  Several
players created 10 runs or better: Dun Laoghaire's Jay Buhner (12.3), 
Hong Kong's Matt Lawton (11.6), Needmore's Gary Sheffield (11.3), 
Ferdinand's John Cangelosi (10.8), Cincinnati's Charles Johnson (10.5)
Ferdinand's Craig Grebeck (10.4), Reykjavik's Sean Berry (10.1) and Grain
Belt's Tim Salmon (10.0).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's MICKEY MORANDINI put up a 0.091 OPS in 22 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Evanston's TERRY MULHOLLAND allowed only 3 runs in 17.0 innings on 14 hits 
and 2 walks while striking out five.  Chinatrust's RANDY JOHNSON threw two 
complete games, allowing 5 runs on 11 hits plus one walk, while striking out 32.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Chinatrust's SCOTT SERVICE allowed only a walk in 4.1 innings while striking
out 7.  Also noteworth were San Francisco's DENNIS ECKERSLEY and Needmore's
ANTONIO OSUNA.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
San Francisco's ESTEBAN LOAIZA gave up 15 runs in 10.0 innings, allowing 16
hits and 5 walks, as well as two homeruns.
