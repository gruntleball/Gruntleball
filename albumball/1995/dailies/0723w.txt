WOWEE ZOWEE! LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending July 23rd


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The CINCINNATI REDS went 6-1 scoring a league-leading 55 runs while
allowing 27, for a margin of +28 runs.  The 6-1 Butt Mountain Beavis
allowed only 17 runs for fewest in the league, edging out 5-2 Grain
Belt by one run.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The TUCKER TOOLS POLICE were 2-5 with an horrific 61 runs allowed,
against 32 runs scored for a margin of -29.  Several other teams were
also 2-5, including the Firsts, Whales, Endorphins, Perfectos and 101ers.
The Needmore Perfectos scored only 18 runs to trail the league.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Cincinnati's RAY LANKFORD led the league with 17.2 runs created in 30
plate appearances.  Close behind him was Century's Todd Hundley with 15.3
RC in only 16 plate appearances, and San Francisco's Carl Everett with 14.6
RC in 25 plate appearances.

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
San Francisco's ROYCE CLAYTON put up a .211 OPS in 30 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Dun Laoghaire's TOM GLAVINE pitched 15.2 innings, allowing only 2 runs on
7 hits (but 9 walks) while striking out 11.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Hong Kong's MARVIN FREEMAN pitched six scoreless innings in relief, allowing
only one hit and walking three.  Cincinnati's Norm Charlton was perfect 
for 3.1 innings with 6 strikeouts.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's Bob Wickman appeared in three games, throwing 3.2 innings and
coughing up 9 runs, including three homeruns.
