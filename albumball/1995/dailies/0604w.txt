WOWEE ZOWEE! LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending June 4th


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Barcelona Brewers were 7-0, and have won 16 straight games.
This week they were the most dominant team in the league, scoring 
37 runs and allowing a mere 14.  The top scoring team was the 
Ferdinand Magellans with 51 runs scored. 

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Tewksbury Bobs went 1-6 and were outscored by 34 runs, 16-50.
Their 16 runs was lowest in the league as well. The Hong Kong
Cavaliers surrendered 62 runs, leading in that category.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's Barry Bonds, by a slim margin, lead the league with 13.0
runs created.  He put up a 379/457/931 line in 35 plate appearances.
Just behind him was Butt Mountain's John Valentin (12.9), followed by
Hong Kong's Ivan Rodriguez (12.0), Butt Mountain's Matt Williams (11.7),
Baraboo's Orlando Miller (10.9), Dun Laoghaire's Edgar Martinez (10.1),
Hong Kong's Vinny Castilla (10.1), and Cincinnati's Barry Larkin (10.0).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Cincinnati's Eric Young's 105 OPS in 19 plate appearances.  San Carlos's
Rich Becker put up a pretty bad 229 OPS in 31 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's Dennis Martinez gave up 3 runs in 17.0 innings on 11 hits and
4 walks while striking out 9.  Tucker's David Cone pitched 16.0 innings 
and allowed 5 runs on 9 hits and 5 walks while striking out 12.
Cincinnati's Hideo Nomo pitched 9.2 innings in two starts and didn't allow
a run.  He gave up 3 hits and 5 walks, striking out 12.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's Dave Veres threw 4.2 perfect innings, striking out 3.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Hong Kong's John Wasdin gave up 13 runs in 4.2 innings spanning six games.
He surrendered 14 hits, including 4 homeruns and 4 walks.
