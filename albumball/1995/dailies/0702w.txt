WOWEE ZOWEE! LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending July 2nd


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The DUN LAOGHAIRE MESSIAHS was 6-1, scoring a league-leading 54 runs and 
allowing 41.  The 5-1 Century Atoms allowed only 15 runs and the 4-2
Ferdinand Magellans scored 50 and allowed only 21.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FELLDOWNHEAD FIRSTS were again 1-5, taking the worst title for the 
third straight week.  They weren't the team with the poorest run 
differential.  That belongs to the 2-5 Tewksbury Bobs who allowed a 
league-worst 59 runs while scoring only 28.  The most feeble offense
belonged to the 2-5 Needmore Perfectos with 14 runs scored.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Ferlin's JIM THOME created 14.5 runs in 30 plate appearances with a 
batting line of 522/633/913.  Runners up were Chinatrust's Chuck Knoblauch
with 11.7 RC, followed by Reykjavik's Sean Berry (11.1) and Dante Bichette
(10.6).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
San Carlos's LENNY HARRIS  put up a .156 OPS in 21 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Ferlin's MIKE MORGAN pitched 16.0 innings, allowing only one run on 7 hits
plus 4 walks while striking out 11.  Also noteworthy was Tom Glavine's one-
hit, one-walk shutout.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Ferlin's GREG HARRIS pitched 4.0 perfect innings, striking out five.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Tewksbury's KEVIN GROSS made two starts, lasted all of 4.0 innings and
allowed 12 runs on 15 hits, including 3 homeruns, plus 3 walks.
