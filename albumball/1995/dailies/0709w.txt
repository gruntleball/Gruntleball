WOWEE ZOWEE! LEAGUE
Weekly Awards for the Week ending July 9th


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The BARCELONA BREWERS went 5-2 and led the league in scoring with 55 runs.
They also had the greatest run margin of 29 runs.  The stingiest pitching
and defense belonged to the Ferdinand Magellans who allowed a mere 17 runs
in 7 games.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FELLDOWNHEAD FIRSTS were 0-7, taking the title for the fourth straight 
week.  They tied for last in runs scored (12), were next to last in runs 
allowed with 54, and by virtue of both, had the poorest run margin in the
league.  Only Hong Kong allowed more runs with 57, altitude-aided.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
A HUGE week for hitters with 11 players creating 10 RC or more.  Grain 
Belt's TIM SALMON nearly obliterated the field with 19.1 RC, followed by
Hong Kong's Frank Thomas with 17.7.  From there, Travis Fryman created
13.2 runs, followed by 13.1 for Chili Davis and 13.0 for Mo Vaughn.
Rounding out the top 11 were Fernando Vina (12.5), Albert Belle (11.3)
Manny Ramirez (11.3), Craig Biggio (11.1), Chuck Knoblauch (11.0) and
Tony Phillips (10.2).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Ferlin's MARK GRUDZIELANEK managed a .105 OPS in 19 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Tewksbury's TOM CANDIOTTI allowed only one run in 17.0 innings, on 11
hits plus 4 walks, while striking out 14.  Also noteworthy was Orel
Hershiser's efforts in which he allowed 1 run on 9 hits and 8 walks,
also in 17.0 innings.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
San Carlos's MARK PETKOVSEK pitched 6.2 shutout innings on three hits
and no walks.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Hong Kong's Frankie Rodriguez allowed 16 runs in 8.0 innings spanning two
starts.  He allowed 21 hits and 5 walks and EIGHT homeruns!
