WEEKLY AWARDS FOR (short) WEEK ENDING AUGUST 11TH

GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Reversing last week's winless record, the Winnetka Baha'i went 7-0, scoring 66
runs (to lead the league) and allowed 33, also leading the league in run margin
with +33.  The 6-1 Butt Mountain Beavis allowed a mere 12 runs in seven games.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The GPCC Shebazzle went 0-7, scoring a league lowest 22 runs and allowing 37.
The 1-6 Felldownhead Firsts gave up 49 runs and tied with the Newfoundland
Perfectos with a -22 run margin.

HITTER OF THE WEEK: 
Freakin' MIKE BORDICK (???) led the league with 21.5 runs created in 35 plate
appearances.  He batted 545/571/1182 with SIX homeruns during the week.  (He
hit five homeruns ALL SEASON in real life!)  To date he is hitting 329/364/579
against a "real" 240/307/318 line.  And half of that was NOT at altitude.  He's
HANDILY outhitting Chipper Jones...

Runners up were Ferdinand's Barry Bonds (16.5), Winnetka's Vinny Castilla (16.2),
Century's Manny Ramirez (14.3), Ferlin's Ellis Burks (13.1) and Winnetka's Ryne
Sandberg (13.0).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
GPCC's Eddie Williams put up a .211 OPS in 19 plate appearances.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Butt Mountain's ROGER CLEMENS allowed one run (zero earned) in 16.0 innings on six
hits plus five walks, striking out 10.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's RICKY BOTTALICO allowed one hit and one walk in 5.0 innings, striking
out five.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Ken Hill's 5-inning, 13-runs allowed does it for me.  19 hits, 2 homeruns, 2 walks.
