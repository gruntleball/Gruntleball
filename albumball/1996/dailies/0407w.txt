WEEKLY AWARDS FOR WEEK ENDING APRIL 7TH

GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Evanston Endorphins were a perfect 7-0, scoring 57 runs and allowing 18.
The Okoboji Famers (5-2) led the league in scoring with 58 runs.  The best
pitching and defense was from the Chinatrust Whales (5-2) who allowed only
14 runs.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Both the Rocky Mountain Oysters and the Garmisch-Partenkirchen Chim-Chim 
Shebazzle! were 0-7, but they went about things in very different manners.
The Oysters only scored 16 runs, worst in the league, but allowed only 31,
which isn't horrible.  The Shebazzle! scored 25 runs, but allowed 69 runs, 
so based upon their run differential, they get the nod for league's worst.
The Winnetka Baha'i allowed 82(!) runs in its seven games.

HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Cincinnati's Ray Lankford led the league in runs created with 18.6.  Ray put
up a batting line of 571/640/1286 in 25 plate appearances.  Runners-up were
Felldownhead's Alex Rodriguez (14.7 RC), Okoboji's Mike Piazza (12.9) and
Winnetka's Ryne Sandberg (12.1).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Baraboo's Jeff Kent had 0.0 RC for the week in 25 plate appearances.  Jeff
was hitless in 23 at bats with two walks.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Chinatrust's Andy Pettitte had this line for the week: 18.0 3 0 0 7 14
'Nuff said.  Also noteworthy was Evanston's Jeff Fassero with 15.0 4 0 0 4 17
and San Carlos's Juan Guzman with 9 2 0 0 1 7.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's Greg McMichael appeared in four games, and pitched 8.0 innings, 
allowing one run on one hit and two walks.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Winnetka's Darryl Kile made two starts, lasted a total of 6.2 innings and gave
up 14 runs on 19 hits (two of them homeruns) and three walks, striking out eight.
