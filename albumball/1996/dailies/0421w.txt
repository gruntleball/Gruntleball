WEEKLY AWARDS FOR WEEK ENDING APRIL 14TH

GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Evanston Endorphins were a perfect 6-0 for the week.  Mr. Henninger's
team scored 32 runs and allowed 15 runs, leading the league in run
prevention, for a margin of +17.  The team with the largest margin was the 
Chinatrust Whales, who scored a league-leading 57 runs and allowed 25.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Four teams were 1-5 this week.  The Ferdinand Magellans scored the fewest
runs with 18, followed by the Rocky Mountain Oysters with 19 runs scored.
The Oysters also allowed a league-worst 51 runs for a margin of -32 runs.

HITTER OF THE WEEK: (A big week for catchers and middle infielders!)
Chinatrust's Chuck Knoblauch created 14.0 runs in 31 plate appearances.  He
put up a batting line of 480/581/880 with 3 HR and 13 runs scored.  Runner
up was Newfoundland's Mark Parent(!) who in only 19 plate appearances 
created 13.9 runs, batting 579/579/1263.  Rounding out the field were Okoboji's
Mike Piazza (12.8), Dun Laoghaire's Jay Buhner (11.9), Butt Mountain's Terry
Steinbach (11.4), Chinatrust's Mike Bordick(!) (11.4), Tewksbury's Royce
Clayton (10.6), Barcelona's Brady Anderson (10.6) and Winnetka's Ivan 
Rodriguez (10.1)  

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Tarboro's Alvaro Espinoza put up a .139 OPS in 23 plate appearances and also
hit into three double plays.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Tarboro's John Smiley threw 17 shutout innings, allowing 12 hits and a walk
while striking out 11.  Also of note were Chinatrust's Tim Wakefield and 
Okoboji's Mike Hampton who each threw two-hit shutouts.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
San Carlos's Dan Naulty and Paul Byrd were both outstanding, combining for 10.2
innings, two hits, three walks and five strikeouts.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's (gad's they're all over the place) Tom Gordon made two starts, throwing
9.2 innings.  He allowed 15 runs on 22 hits plus six walks (no home runs though).
