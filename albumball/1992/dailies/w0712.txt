SLANTED & ENCHANTED
Weekly Awards for the week ending July 12th, 1992

Good Team of the Week
The Ferlin Huskies were 5-0 and outscored their opposition by a league-leading 15
runs.  The Chinatrust Whales led the league in offense with 34 runs (go figure),
and the Cincinnati Reds allowed just 9 runs in six games.

Bad Team of the Week
The Houston Streets went 0-5, scoring 10 runs, allowing 21.  The Century Atoms had
the poorest run margin at -15 runs.  Baraboo allowed 30 runs in just four games.

Hitter of the Week
Grain Belt's Ron Gant created 7.4 runs in 5 games with a 1.201 OPS.  Keith Miller
was runner up with 7.2 RC and did so in just 11 PA.  Finally, Randy Velarde created
7.0 runs in just 4 plate appearances.

Bad Hitter of the Week
Needmore's Scott Hemond created 0.0 runs with a .125 OPS in 16 plate appearances.

Starter of the Week
Cincinnati's Andy Benes made two starts totaling 16.0 innings.  He allowed 8 hits
and no walks, while striking out 14.  Honorable mentions to Reykjavik's Mark
Gubicza, and Chinatrust's Bill Swift and Kevin Tapani.

Reliever of the Week
Dun Laoghaire's Mike Maddux threw 6.0 innings, allowing 3 hits, no walks, and he
struck out 6.

Bad pitcher of the Week 
Century's Scott Sanderson made two starts, totaling 7.0 innings.  He gave up 10
runs on 12 hits and 3 walks.
