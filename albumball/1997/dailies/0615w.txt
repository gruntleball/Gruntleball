BRIGHTEN THE CORNERS LEAGUE

WEEKLY AWARDS FOR WEEK ENDING JUNE 15TH

GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The CHINATRUST WHALES (Rob Miller) went 7-0 for the week, scoring 47 runs and 
allowing 29, for a run margin of +18.  The 5-2 Century Atoms (Randy Stearns)
led the league in scoring (51) and in run margin (+29).  The 6-1 Cincinnati
Reds (Chuck Miller) allowed only 15 runs for the week.


BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The SAN CARLOS QUENTINS (John Manuel) went 1-6 during the week, scoring 37 runs
and allowing a league high 74 for a margin of -37.  The 1-5 Dun Laoghaire 
Messiahs (Ron Potter) scored a mere 15 runs and had the poorest run margin at
-39 for the week.


HITTER OF THE WEEK: 
In a big week for offense, Winnetka's LARRY WALKER created 19.3 runs over 30
plate appearances with a batting line of 538/600/1192.  Just behind him was
Tarboro's Tino Martinez (17.7 RC).  The remaining players in double-digit RC
were Century's Bernie Williams (13.8), Newfoundland's Ken Griffey (11.7), 
Tarboro's Rondell White (11.4), Chinatrust's Mike Stanley (11.4) and San 
Carlos's Brian Giles (10.3).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Garmisch-Partenkirchen's RAY DURHAM batted 083/120/083 for the week in 25 plate
appearances.  Cincinnati's Doug Glanville was right behind him with 27 PA and 
a 080/148/080 line.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Century's PAT HENTGEN finally had a start that begins to justify his contract, 
throwing a complet game shutout and allowing two hits and a walk while striking
out eight.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Garmisch-Partenkirchen's JASON CHRISTIANSEN threw 4.2 innings, allowing one hit
and one walk, striking out five.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Century's WOODY WILLIAMS made two starts, amassing 7.1 innings of work in which
he allowed 12 runs on 14 hits plus five walks.
