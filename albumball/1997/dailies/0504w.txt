BRIGHTEN THE CORNERS LEAGUE

WEEKLY AWARDS FOR WEEK ENDING MAY 4TH

GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Evanston Endorphins were a perfect 7-0 for the week, and the Fallkirk
Frightened Rabbit was 6-0.  Fallkirk was slightly more dominant, with a +22
margin in six games vs. 27 runs in 7 games for Evanston.  Evanston did lead
the league in scoring, with 54 runs, while Fallkirk's pitching has started
to come around, allowing only 14 runs in six games.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Wow. Three 0-7 teams this week.  Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Okoboji and San 
Carlos.  Garmisch-Partenkirchen had the poorest run margin, scoring 17 runs
and allowing 59, which was the most allowed.   Only Dun Laoghaire's offense
was more inept, scoring only 15 runs in their 7 games.  


HITTER OF THE WEEK: 
Tarboro's MOISES ALOU created 16.3 runs in his 7 games with a batting line 
of 462/588/1000.  Runners-up were Fallkirk's John Valentin (12.8) and Al 
Martin (12.4 in 2 games, 11 PA), Evanston's Raul Mondesi (11.3), Tewksbury's 
Nomar Garciaparra (10.8), Felldownhead's Jeff Reed (10.2) and Ferdinand's 
Craig Biggio (10.1).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Dun Laoghaire's Brian McRae actually had a negative RC.  To go with his .245
OPS, he got caught stealing twice, thus negating any benefit of the four times
he DID reach base this week.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's Pedro Martinez threw two complete game shutouts, allowing six hits
and five walks, while striking out 19.  On the bright side, he's getting used
up quickly throwing all those complete games.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Century's John Thomson threw 7.1 innings of one hit ball, striking out five.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
LaTroy Hawkins made four appearances and allowed 10 runs on 8 hits and 4 walks...
in 1.2 innings.
