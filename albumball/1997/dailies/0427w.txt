BRIGHTEN THE CORNERS LEAGUE

WEEKLY AWARDS FOR WEEK ENDING APRIL 27TH

GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
In the "I'd rather be lucky than good" department, both the Chinatrust Whales
and the Ferdinand Magellans went 4-1 this week with run margins of +7 and +3, 
respectively.  The 4-2 Barcelona Brewers were the most dominant with a 17-run 
margin, followed by Baraboo at +14.  The 3-3 Winnetka Baha'i feature the most
potent offense, scoring 41 runs.  The 4-2 Tarboro Goobers were the most stingy,
allowing only 18 runs.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The Dun Laoghaire Messiahs went 1-5 again, and again had the poorest run margin 
at -20.  They scored the fewest runs (12) as well.  The Newfoundland Perfectos
allowed 39 runs to "lead" the league in that category.


HITTER OF THE WEEK: 
Only three hitters made double-digit runs created this week.  Winnetka's Larry
Walker led with 11.3 RC, followed by Newfoundland's Ken Griffey (10.7) and
Baraboo's Jeff Bagwell (10.0).

BAD HITTER OF THE WEEK:
Zzyzx's Matt Stairs started four games and managed a whopping .105 OPS in 17
plate appearances.  Felldownhead's Patrick Lennon and Evanston's Fernando Vina
also scored high on the futility index.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's Pedro Martinez threw a complete game 3-hit shutout, walking nobody 
and striking out nine.

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's Mike Dejean allowed but a walk in 3.1 scoreless innings.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Century's John Thomson allowed 9 runs on 13 hits (3 homeruns) and three walks
in 4.2 innings.
