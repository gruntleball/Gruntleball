Brighten the Corners League
Monthly Awards for May 1997


Good Team of the Month:
The FELLDOWNHEAD FIRSTS went 22-5, followed by the 21-7 Baraboo Goitermen and
the 19-8 Ferlin Huskies.  Felldownhead scored 185 runs, second to Winnetka's
altitude-aided 187, and allowed 109, fourth best in the league.  The Baraboo
Goitermen surrendered the fewest runs (88), followed by the 14-14 Tarboro 
Goobers with 90.   Baraboo also had the strongest run margin at +94.

Bad Team of the Month:
Both the Garmisch-Partenkirchen Chim-Chim Shebazzle and the Newfoundland
Perfectos went 7-20 for the month.  Newfoundland had a slightly poorer run
margin (-51) so they get the award.  The Shebazzle only scored 100 runs for
the month.  The 8-19 Rocky Mountain Oyster allowed the most runs, (197) and had
the poorest run margin at -96.

Hitter of the Month:
Ferlin's  JIM THOME lead the league with 39.4 runs created.  The remaining 30+
RC players were Winnetka's Larry Walker (34.1), Baraboo's Tony Gwynn (32.8),
Winnetka's Frank Thomas (32.8) and Felldownhead's Jeff Reed (30.7).

Bad Hitter of the Month:
Garmisch-Partenkirchen's ARCHI CIANFROCCO created 3.1 runs for the month, 
batting 186/260/214, going 13 for 70.

Starter of the Month:
Baraboo's KEVIN TAPANI pitched 33.0 innings in four starts with one complete 
game.  He had a 0.55 ERA, allowing 4 runs on 20 hits and five walks, striking
out 29.

Reliever of the Month:
Tewksbury's JOHN WETTELAND pitched 12.1 innings with a 0.00 ERA.  In 12 games,
he gave up no runs on 6 hits plus 4 walks, striking out 10.

Bad Pitcher of the Month:
Rocky Mountain's TODD JONES gave up 22 runs in 13.2 innings, on 25 hits plus 7
walks.
