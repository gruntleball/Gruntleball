queens of the stone age league
weekly awards for the week ending september 13


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The TEWKSBURY BOBS (James Bailey) went 6-1, leading the league with 57
runs scored and tied with the 5-2 Dun Laoghaire Messiahs (Ron Potter) 
in run prevention with 19.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FALLKIRK FRIGHTENED RABBIT (James Scott) were 1-6, scoring 26 runs
while allowing 46.  Though having the worst record, they were not worst
in runs scored, allowed or margin.  The worst offense was from the 2-4 
Garmisch-Partenkirchen Shebazzle! (Andrew Bernstein) with 17 runs scored.   
The poorest pitching and defense was from the 2-5 Felldownhead Firsts
(Doug English) which allowed 72 runs and had a -41 run margin.

Tewksbury's MICKEY MORANDINI created 14.8 runs in 31 plate appearances
with a batting line of 480/581/960 with three homeruns.  Runners-up

Tewksbury's Nomar Garciaparra (11.7) and Rocky Mountain's Shannon
Stewart (11.7).

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Century's ROLANDO ARROJO saved 4.9 runs above average, pitching 15 innings
and allowing 1 run on 9 hits plus 4 walks with 1 homerun.  Right behind
were Evanston's Dwight Gooden (4.8) and Tewksbury's Darren Dreifort (4.6).

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Okoboji's TIM WORRELL saved 3.0 runs above average, pitching 9.1 innings,
allowing 3 runs on 5 hits plus 2 walks, striking out 11.  Following him 
were Barcelona's Rudy Seanez (2.8), Ferdinand's Greg McMichael (2.8) and
Bryce Florie (2.7).

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Felldownhead's RICK KRIVDA cost his team 9.4 runs more than average. 
Also having poor weeks were teammate Doug Bochtler (-8.8) and Okoboji's
Blake Stein (-8.2).
