queens of the stone age league
weekly awards for the week ending june 14


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Both the EVANSTON ENDORPHINS (Thom Henninger) and Barcelona Brewers  (Al
Marshall) were 5-1.  Evanston was slightly more dominant, outscoring 
their opponents by 16 runs compared to 13 runs for Barcelona.  The 5-2
Dun Laoghaire Messiahs scored the most runs (65) and were the most 
dominant by far (+42), allowing 23 runs in seven games.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FALLKIRK FRIGHTENED RABBIT (James Scott) went 1-6, scoring 19 runs 
while allowing a league-high 74 runs resulting in a league-worst 
margin of -55.  The McCullers Lancers (John Manuel) also went 1-6, 
scoring 26 runs and allowing 41.  The 1-5 Chinatrust Whales scored only 
16 runs, the fewest in the league.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Dun Laoghaire's ANDY ASHBY saved his team 6.7 runs, pitching 17 innings,
allowing 2 runs on 8 hits and 5 walks, striking out 13.  Runners up were
Dun Laoghaire's Greg Maddux (5.5) and Cincinnati's Scott Erickson (4.6).

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Tarboro's JOHN FRANCO pitched 5.0 perfect innings, saving 2.5 runs above
average.  Runners up were Ferdinand's Tom Gordon and Barcelona's Willie
Banks, who each saved 2.0 runs above average.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Fallkirk's JAIME NAVARRO (Navawful) cost his team 8.6 runs more than a
league average pitcher, allowing 8 runs in 1.2 innings, including 3 home
runs.  Teammate Jose Mesa was nearly as bad at -8.4 runs, followed by 
Tarboro teammates Jay Powell (-8.1) and Brett Tomko (-8.0).
