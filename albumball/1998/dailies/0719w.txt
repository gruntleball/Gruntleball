GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The NEWFOUNDLAND PERFECTOS (John Sasman) went 6-0, scoring 45 runs and 
giving up 21 runs for a margin of +24.  The 5-1 Tarboro Goobers (Will
Lingo) lead the league in scoring (47), run prevention (16) and margin
(of course, +31).

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The ROCKY MOUNTAIN OYSTERS (Haverty/Mojo) went 1-5,scoring 23 runs and
allowing 38 for a margin of -15.  The 2-4 Okoboji Cropdusters (Bryan
Smith) scored the fewest runs (12).  The 3-3 Cincinnati Reds (Chuck
Miller) and 2-4 Ferdinand Magellans (Mat Olkin) allowed the most runs
(43).  The 2-4 Century Atoms (Randy Stearns) secured the worst margin
with 22 scored and 40 allowed for a -18.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's CURT SCHILLING saved 7.2 runs above average.  In his two 
starts, he threw 17 innings, allowing 2 runs on 8 hits, 1 walk and 
struck out 18.  Runners up were McCullers Kenny Rogers (5.1)and Dun 
Laoghaire's Greg Maddux (4.2).

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's VALERIO DE LOS SANTOS saved 2.2 runs above average, pitching
4.1 perfect innings.  Runners-up were Cincinnati's Hideo Nomo (2.1), 
Fallkirk's Dan Serafini (2.0) and Chinatrust's Paul Quantrill (2.0).

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Century's JOHN THOMSON cost his team 14.1 runs above average with his
one plus innings in which he surrendered 10 hits, 2 homeruns and 3 walks.
Baraboo's Kevin Tapani was also pretty bad, costing his team 10.4 runs.
