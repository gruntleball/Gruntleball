queens of the stone age league
weekly awards for the week ending july 5


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The NEWFOUNDLAND PERFECTOS (John Sasman) went 6-1, scoring 48 runs 
and allowing 29 for a league-leading margin of +19.  The 5-2 
Chinatrust Whales scored 50 runs to lead the league in offense, 
while the 3-4 Evanston Endorphins (Thom Henninger) allowed only 21
runs. 

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FALLKIRK FRIGHTENED RABBIT (James Scott) went 0-7, scoring 27 runs 
while allowing 68 for a margin of -41, the latter two figures being 
league worsts.  Both the Evanston Endorphins and 1-6 Felldownhead Firsts
(Doug English) scored only 20 runs.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's CURT SCHILLING saved his team 5.3 runs, pitching 15.0 
innings and allowing only 2 runs on 10 hits plus 1 walk, striking
out 19.  Runners-up were Garmisch-Partenkirchen's Ramiro Mendoza (4.9),
Newfoundland's Ramon Martinez (4.9) and Newfoundland's Brian Moehler 
(4.2).

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Cincinnati's SCOTT SULLIVAN saved 2.8 runs, pitching 6.0 innings, 
allowing a run on 1 hit and 1 walk.  Runners-up were Felldownhead's
Steve Kline (2.7), and a bunch of guys with 2.2 runs saved: Evanston's
Jerry Spradlin, Chinatrust's Matt Anderson, Ferlin's Mike Cather,
Fallkirk's Julian Tavarez and Tarboro's Vic Darensbourg.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Fallkirk's ANDY PETTITTE had a dismal week.  In 5.2 innings he allowed
19 runs, all earned, on 18 hits, 8 walks and allowed 2 homeruns, costing
his team 14.9 runs.  Also having rough weeks were Evanston's Carlos
Perez (-7.4), Ferdinand's Jaret Wright (-7.3) and Ferlin's Sidney Ponson
(-7.2).
