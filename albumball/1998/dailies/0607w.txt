GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The TEWKSBURY BOBS (James Bailey) and the Cincinnati Reds (Chuck Miller)
were both 6-1 for the week.  Tewksbury scored 51 runs and allowed a 
league low 22 runs for a +29 margin, best in the league.  The 5-2 
Ferdinand Magellans (Mat Olkin) led the league in scoring with 58 runs.  

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FALLKIRK FRIGHTENED RABBIT (James Scott) went 0-7, scoring a league-
low 19 runs while allowing 40 for a margin of -21.  The 1-6 Garmisch-
Partenkirchen Chim Chim Shebazzle! (Andrew Bernstein) allowed a league-
high 53 runs and had the worst run margin of -26.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Rocky Mountain's ROGER CLEMENS pitched 13.0 innings, allowing 1 run on
8 hits and 4 walks, striking out 14.  He prevented 4.4 runs above 
average.  Runners up were Ferlin's Kirk Rueter and Kevin Brown (3.7 and
3.2 runs saved, respectively), and Fallkirk's Chris Haney (3.1).

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Ferdinand's CURT LESKANIC saved 2.6 runs above average, pitching 6.1
innings of shutout ball, allowing 2 hits and 2 walks and striking out 6.
Tieing him was Fallkirk's JULIAN TAVAREZ who threw 5.3 shutout innings,
allowing 1 hit and 2 walks.

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Garmisch-Partenkirchen's MIKE SIROTKA cost his team 9.4 runs above 
average.  He pitched 13 innings, allowing 16 runs on 24 hits.  Nearly as
bad was Ferdinand's Esteban Yan (-9.1) who gave up 15 runs in 5.7 
innings.
