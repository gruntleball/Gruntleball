queens of the stone age league
weekly awards for the week ending september 27


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Once it no longer mattered, the CHINATRUST WHALES (Rob Miller) had 
their best week of the year, going 6-0 and scoring 31 runs while
allowing 17.  The 3-3 Winnetka Baha'i led in offense with 51 runs
scored followed by 4-2 Dun Laoghaire with 50.  Chinatrust led in
run prevention, giving up only 17 runs.  Dun Laoghaire allowed 20
runs and had a margin of +30.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
Three teams were 1-5 with the FALLKIRK FRIGHTENED RABBIT (James
Scott) getting trounced most definitively, scoring 20 and allowing
44 runs.  The other 1-5 teams were the Ferlin Huskies (23-41) and
the Okoboji Cropdusters (28-37).  Winnetka allowed the most runs
with 48.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Barcelona's PEDRO MARTINEZ saved 6.2 runs above average, pitching 14.2
innings, allowing 3 runs on 7 hits, no walks and striking out 15.  Runners-
up were Chinatrust's Brian Boehringer (4.5), Dun Laoghaire's Tom Glavine 
(4.3) and Sean Bergman (3.7).

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Chinatrust's BRAD WOODALL saved 2.6 runs, pitching six innings of 1-run
ball, allowing 2 hits, no walks.  Runners up were Okoboji's Tim Worrell
(2.5) and Rocky Mountain's Scott Eyre (2.0).

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:
Winnetka's Kent Bottenfield surrendered 8.9 runs worse than average. He
threw 11.2 innings, allowing 16 runs on 20 hits, 5 walks.  Close behind 
was Evanston's Hideki Irabu with -8.3 runs.
