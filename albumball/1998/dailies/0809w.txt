queens of the stone age league
weekly awards for the week ending august 9


GOOD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The EVANSTON ENDORPHINS (Thom Henninger), Dún Laoghaire Messiahs (Ron 
Potter) and Ferdinand Magellans (Mat Olkin) went 5-1 for the week. 
Ferdinand was outscored by 3 runs for the week.  Evanston scored 33 runs
 and allowed 18 for a margin of +15, which was the best of those three 
teams.  The 4-2 Tewksbury Bobs led the league with 58 runs scored and 
with a margin of +22.  Dún Laoghaire led the league in run prevention 
with 17.

BAD TEAM OF THE WEEK:
The FELLDOWNHEAD FIRSTS (Doug English) went 1-6, scoring 25 runs and 
giving up 37.  Both the 2-4 Fallkirk Frightened Rabbit and 2-4 Rocky
Mountain Oysters scored a mere 20 runs.  The 3-3 Cincinnati Reds gave up
the most runs (45).  And Fallkirk had the poorest run margin of -21.

STARTER OF THE WEEK:
Evanston's STEVE WOODARD saved 6.6 runs above average, pitching 17 
innings and giving up only 3 runs on 10 hits with no walks.  Runners-up
were Barcelona's Curt Schilling (5.4), Century's Francisco Cordova (5.2)
and Dun Laoghaire's Pete Harnisch (5.1).

RELIEVER OF THE WEEK:
Chinatrust's DOUG JONES saved 3.3 runs above average, pitching 7 
scoreless innings of 1-hit ball, walking 1 and striking out 5.  Next

BAD PITCHER OF THE WEEK:

Mountain's Jay Witasick allowed 11.5 runs worse than average.
